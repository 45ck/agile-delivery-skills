# Sample Flow

## Scenario
A product team keeps overcommitting during sprints. Large stories are dragged in half-defined, regressions recur, and each review ends with a pile of carryover work.

## Suggested pack flow
1. `backlog-groomer`  
   Review upcoming items for ambiguity, hidden dependencies, and readiness before planning.

2. `story-splitting-advisor`  
   Break oversized stories into thinner, user-visible slices that can complete within the sprint.

3. `sprint-goal-writer`  
   Turn the selected work into a coherent sprint goal instead of a list of unrelated tickets.

4. `acceptance-driven-planner`  
   Anchor implementation around acceptance criteria, edge cases, and test focus areas.

5. `definition-of-done-enforcer`  
   Check whether items that look “finished” actually satisfy review, test, and readiness expectations.

6. `regression-discipline-checker`  
   Review whether repeated breakage is being prevented or merely repaired each sprint.

7. `iteration-outcome-reviewer`  
   Compare the sprint goal with actual delivered outcomes and carryover.

8. `retrospective-pattern-finder`  
   Find recurring causes such as weak slicing, hidden blockers, or unstable regression protection.

## Expected artifacts
- groomed backlog table
- split-story plan
- sprint goal statement
- acceptance-led delivery notes
- done/not-done checklist result
- regression-discipline review
- iteration outcome summary
- retrospective pattern table
